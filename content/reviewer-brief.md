# Reviewer Brief — Patek Philippe Collector Series

**From:** Editor  
**To:** Reviewer  
**Date:** 2026-04-06

## What matters most

This series is for collectors, so accuracy matters at the level of references, movements, provenance language, originality, service implications, and market framing. Please review drafts with a bias toward precision over elegance whenever the two conflict.

## Review priorities

1. **Factual accuracy**
   - Verify reference numbers, calibers, complication descriptions, launch-era claims, and family-level distinctions.
   - Flag soft claims that sound true but are not properly sourced.

2. **Collector-risk language**
   - Watch for unsupported claims around rarity, investment performance, authenticity, originality, and value retention.
   - Require hedged wording where certainty is not defensible.

3. **Condition and originality terminology**
   - Make sure terms like *unpolished*, *original dial*, *service dial*, *extract*, *full set*, and *matching* are used correctly.
   - Flag wording that could mislead a buyer about what documentation actually proves.

4. **Source quality**
   - Prefer official brand references, auction catalogs/results, and reputable specialist sources.
   - Ask for stronger sourcing when a claim leans on forum consensus or dealer copy.

5. **HTML and structure sanity check**
   - Confirm drafts are publishable as HTML and not markdown pasted into tags.
   - Check heading hierarchy, lists, and tables for usability.

## Fact-check expectations

- Highlight any claim that cannot be supported from the cited source.
- Distinguish between verified facts and market interpretation.
- Add reviewer notes where dates, references, production quantities, or configuration details may vary by source.
- If a statement is commonly repeated but difficult to verify, recommend a rewrite rather than letting it stand.

## Escalate immediately if you see

- Claims that a watch is authentic or original without enough evidence
- Unsupported market-value promises
- Reference/caliber mismatches
- Auction anecdotes presented as broad market truth
- HTML that will cause publishing cleanup work

## Review output format

For each draft, provide:

- **Accuracy verdict:** pass / revise / high-risk
- **Top factual corrections:** bullet list
- **Source gaps:** bullet list
- **Collector-risk phrasing to soften:** bullet list
- **HTML or structure issues:** bullet list

If a piece is strong but has two or three dangerous sentences, call that out directly. Those are the mistakes most likely to damage trust.
