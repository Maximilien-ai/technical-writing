## 🔴 Factual Errors (Must Fix)

### 1. Barbaresco Communes — San Rocco Seno d'Elvio Is Not a Separate Commune

**Draft says:** "Four communes — Barbaresco, Neive, Treiso, and San Rocco Seno d'Elvio — share about 790 hectares."

**Problem:** San Rocco Seno d'Elvio was absorbed into the municipality of **Alba** in 1929. It is not a separate commune. The Consorzio's own website states the production zone covers "the municipalities of Barbaresco, Treiso, Neive and **part of the municipality of Alba**."

**Fix:** "Three communes — Barbaresco, Neive, and Treiso — plus a portion of the municipality of Alba (the former San Rocco Seno d'Elvio) share about [X] hectares."

**Source:** Consorzio di Tutela Barolo Barbaresco Alba Langhe e Dogliani (langhevini.it/en/barbaresco-docg/)

### 2. Geological Age Labels Are Reversed

**Draft says:** "**Tortonian soils** — named for the Tortonian age of the Miocene epoch — are older, more compacted formations..." and "**Helvetian soils** — from the earlier Helvetian age — are younger and looser."

**Problem:** The Tortonian epoch (11.6–7.2 Ma) is *more recent* than the Helvetian/Serravallian (~13.8–11.6 Ma). The draft describes Tortonian as "older" and Helvetian as "younger" — this is backwards. The Tortonian is the younger geological age; the Helvetian is the older one.

The *characteristics* attributed to each soil type (compact/dense for Serralunga vs. calcareous/loose for La Morra) are correct and well-described. It's the age labels that are wrong.

**Fix:** Remove the "older"/"younger" descriptors or correct them. The soil characteristics should drive the description, not geological age comparisons. Suggested rewrite:

> **Tortonian soils** — named for the Tortonian age of the Miocene epoch — are compacted formations of blue-grey marl and sandstone...
> **Helvetian soils** — from the earlier Helvetian age — are looser: calcareous marl with more calcium carbonate, lighter in color...

**Note:** There is genuine inconsistency in wine literature about which geological label maps to which commune. The standard wine convention (Tortonian → Serralunga; Helvetian → La Morra) is widely used but may be geologically imprecise. Recommend verifying against the Consorzio's geological survey materials or Kerin O'Keefe's *Barolo and Barbaresco* for the authoritative wine-world framing. The safest approach: describe the soil characteristics accurately and note that wine literature uses these geological epoch names as shorthand.

## 🟡 Minor Accuracy Issues (Should Fix)

### 4. Barolo Aging — Clarify the Counting Convention

**Draft says:** "38 months (18 in wood)"

Wikipedia cites "36 months after the harvest." The Consorzio says "at least three years beyond the two months following the harvest." Both are correct — the difference is the starting point (November 1 of harvest year vs. harvest date). The draft's 38 months matches the disciplinare's convention (counted from November 1).

**Recommendation:** Add a brief parenthetical or footnote: "38 months from November 1 of the harvest year" — this prevents confusion for readers who've seen "36 months" elsewhere.

### 5. Bruno Giacosa's Vineyard Name

**Draft says:** "*Santo Stefano di Neive*"

**The cru MGA name is simply *Santo Stefano*** (located in Neive). "di Neive" is informal disambiguation, not the official vineyard designation. For an article that emphasizes precision, use the correct cru name.

**Fix:** "*Santo Stefano* (in Neive)" or just "*Santo Stefano*"

### 6. Price Ranges Need Source or Caveat

**Draft says:** "$35-60 USD" for Barbaresco, "$70-150 USD" for Barolo.

These are plausible but unverified. Prices shift. Consider adding "at time of writing" or citing a specific retailer/index for defensibility.
