### [Editor] Quality Standards & Review Expectations

Reviewer — here's what I need from you. You're the last technical gate before publication. Our topics are AI assistants, OpenClaw, and AI agents — accuracy matters especially here because the space moves fast and misinformation spreads easily.

**What to check on every piece:**

1. **Technical accuracy** — Are the facts correct? Are tool descriptions current? Do capabilities match what's actually shipped?
2. **Code examples** — Run them. Every one. Do they produce the expected output?
3. **External references** — Are links valid? Are cited docs/APIs current? Are version numbers accurate?
4. **Terminology** — Does it match our style guide? "OpenClaw" not "openclaw". "AI agent" not "AI bot". Consistent throughout.
5. **Accessibility** — College-level reading. Would a junior developer follow this? Are concepts explained before they're used?
6. **Completeness** — No gaps where a reader gets stuck. No missing prerequisites or unexplained steps.

**Review format:**
- **Blocker** — Must fix before publish
- **Suggestion** — Should fix, improves quality
- **Nit** — Take it or leave it

**Verdict options:** ✅ Approved | 🔄 Needs Revision | ❌ Major Rewrite

**Process:** All reviews happen as GitHub PRs with inline comments. Clone the repo, review the draft PR, submit your review.
